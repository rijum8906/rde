# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

RDE is built by and for a community of contributors and users who value respectful, constructive collaboration. We are committed to making participation in this project — through issues, pull requests, discussions, or any other project space — a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Expected Behavior

- Communicate with courtesy, even in disagreement. Critique ideas and code, not people.
- Be receptive to differing viewpoints and experiences, especially from contributors newer to the project or to Rust/Linux systems programming generally.
- Give and accept constructive feedback gracefully.
- Take responsibility for mistakes, apologize when warranted, and focus on what was learned.
- Prioritize what is best for the project and its users as a whole.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, insults, or derogatory comments, whether in public project spaces or private communication related to the project.
- Personal or political attacks.
- Publishing others' private information (physical or email address, etc.) without explicit permission.
- Sexualized language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention or advances.
- Sustained disruption of discussions, issues, or pull requests.
- Any other conduct that a reasonable person would consider inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces — the repository (issues, pull requests, discussions, wiki), associated chat channels, and any other forum created by the project team — as well as when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces (e.g. posting from an official project account or acting as an appointed representative at an event).

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainer, Riju Mondal, via the contact method listed on the [GitHub profile](https://github.com/rijum8906). All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly, and the identity of the reporter will be kept confidential to the extent possible.

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards of acceptable behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful. This may include, depending on severity:

1. **Correction** — a private, written warning explaining the violation and why it was inappropriate.
2. **Warning** — a formal warning with consequences for continued behavior, potentially including a temporary interaction restriction.
3. **Temporary Ban** — a temporary ban from any interaction or public communication with the project for a specified period.
4. **Permanent Ban** — a permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the project.

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct was written for the RDE project, drawing on common practices established across the open-source community for handling conduct in collaborative technical projects.
